We've collected 10 of the most fascinating facts about our avian friends and compiled them here.
Did you know that the Sword-billed Hummingbird is the only bird with a bill longer than its body?
Did you know that owls cannot swivel their eyes? Instead they move their heads completely around to see straight behind them.
Did you know that the only bird with nostrils at the end of its beak is the kiwi? This placement helps it sniff for food, such as worms and insects on the ground.
Mockingbirds can imitate many sounds, from a squeaking door to a cat meowing.
Chickens that lay brown eggs have red ear lobes. There is a genetic link between the two.
Crows have the largest cerebral hemispheres (brains), relative to body size, of any avian family.
The human ear, in fact, is a machine to hear birds.
A bird's head is shaped like a cow's head.
A bird cannot fly.
